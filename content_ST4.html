CAUSES OF CLIMATE CHANGE

1. Greenhouse Gas Emissions

Main gases: Carbon dioxide (CO₂), methane (CH₄), nitrous oxide (N₂O)

Sources: Burning fossil fuels (coal, oil, gas), transportation, power plants, deforestation, agriculture

2. Deforestation

Fewer trees = less CO₂ absorbed from the atmosphere

Often cleared for farming, logging, or urban development

3. Industrial Activities

Factories release large amounts of CO₂ and other pollutants

Cement production, chemical manufacturing, etc.

4. Agriculture

Methane from livestock (especially cows)

Nitrous oxide from fertilizers

5. Waste Management

Landfills release methane

Poor waste disposal practices add to emissions

EFFECTS OF CLIMATE CHANGE

1. Rising Temperatures

  • More frequent and intense heatwaves
  • Global average temperature steadily increasing

2. Melting Ice and Rising Sea Levels

  • Arctic and Antarctic ice sheets melting
  • Flooding in coastal areas

3. Extreme Weather Events

  • Stronger hurricanes, cyclones, floods, and droughts
  • More unpredictable weather patterns

4. Ecosystem Disruption

  • Coral bleaching due to warmer oceans
  • Species extinction and habitat loss

5. Impact on Agriculture

  • Crop failure due to droughts or floods
  • Food insecurity in many regions

6. Human Health Issues

  • Spread of diseases (e.g. malaria, dengue)
  • Heatstroke and respiratory problems from pollution
