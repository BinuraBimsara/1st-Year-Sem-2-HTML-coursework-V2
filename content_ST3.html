The Role of the United Nations in Combating Climate Change

1. Global Agreements and Frameworks

The United Nations has been instrumental in establishing international agreements to address climate change. The most significant is the Paris Agreement, adopted in 2015, where nations committed to limiting global warming to well below 2°C, preferably to 1.5°C. Earlier, the Kyoto Protocol laid the foundation for binding emission targets for developed countries.

2. Key UN Bodies Involved

Several UN agencies and programs work together on climate change. These include:

  • UNFCCC (United Nations Framework Convention on Climate Change) – the main platform for negotiations.
  • IPCC (Intergovernmental Panel on Climate Change) – provides scientific assessments on climate change.
  • UNEP (United Nations Environment Programme) – supports implementation and monitoring.

3. Climate Action in the Sustainable Development Goals

Climate Action is the 13th goal in the UN’s Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs). SDG 13 urges countries to strengthen resilience, improve education, and integrate climate measures into policies. It is interconnected with goals on clean energy, life below water, and poverty reduction.

4. Financing Climate Solutions

The UN helps mobilize funds for developing countries to tackle climate change through mechanisms like the Green Climate Fund (GCF). These resources support clean energy projects, disaster preparedness, and adaptation programs in vulnerable regions.

5. Youth and Global Awareness Campaigns

Through global campaigns such as ActNow and Youth Climate Summit, the UN encourages public participation and raises awareness. Young activists are given platforms to speak at high-level events and collaborate on climate solutions globally.

6. Monitoring and Reporting Progress

The UN ensures accountability by requiring countries to submit regular reports on their emissions and climate actions. The Global Stocktake process reviews collective progress toward climate goals and promotes transparency and improvement.
